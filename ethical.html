Artificial Intelligence has been the topic of the future for quite a while now. It is used every day in various ways, whether it is in facial recognition for your phone, in streaming sites that are constantly using the data from the user by using machine learning algorithms, or in Surveillance Cameras, which is the topic that I have chosen and will be reflecting on.

AI Surveillance Cameras have Artificial Intelligence Software built into the cameras which can detect objects e.g., cars, trucks, bikes, humans, and faces. More and more countries are using AI-based surveillance which is mostly supplied by the United States and China. It is shown that at least 75 out of 176 countries are actively using AI-based surveillance.

The problem that worries people the most is the dangers of AI-based surveillance. They can pose a major threat to our privacy. The difference between normal cameras and AI-based cameras is that normal cameras only record objects in motion while AI-based cameras analyse them. AI-based cameras also can allow authorities to go back and search through thousands and thousands of video footage and find what they are specifically looking for with a simple filtered search. This turns our normal security cameras into the smartest AI security guards that will always be watching us at every moment. This type of technology can easily be put into the wrong hands which pose a major threat to our future where we feel constantly monitored.

However, when AI-based surveillance cameras are used for the right reasons and the right purposes, they could be beneficial as well. It could help the Police catch criminals much faster than they can without it. According to the FBI, in 2019, over 73% of property crimes in the US leads to a loss of billions of dollars, most of which could have been prevented with AI-based surveillance. Unlike the average human, the cameras can run 24/7 and have real-time threat detection so they can alert officers on duty much faster.
